Neighborhood Guide

Best Areas to Live Near UCR

Where you live near UCR shapes your whole year — your commute, your rent, how quiet your street is, and whether you need a car. This is a real, local breakdown of every neighborhood UCR students actually rent in, with honest walk times and who each area suits.

Most UCR off-campus housing sits in a handful of neighborhoods spread around the south and east of campus. The biggest factors that separate them are walk time to campus, price, and how "student-friendly" the area is. As a rough rule of thumb, anything under a 30-minute walk is considered walkable, with the sweet spot being around 20 minutes — which on a scooter is closer to 5 minutes. Beyond that, you'll really want a bike, scooter, or car.

When students talk about "campus," they usually mean Orbach Library, the HUB, the SRC (Student Recreation Center), or the central campus core. We've used Orbach as the reference point for walk times below, since it's central and a common destination.

The Most Sought-After Area

Belvedere

Belvedere is the most sought-after neighborhood for UCR students, and the most competitive — it's the closest walkable cluster of houses to campus. It breaks into a few distinct sections, which matter a lot for both walk time and price:

🚶
Belvedere south of Big Springs (newest builds)
The most convenient and highest-priced section — the further south you go, the newer the houses. Streets like Broadbent, Barrett, and Watkins/Big Springs run about a 12–14 minute walk to Orbach, right by Glen Mor and Pentland. This is the premium option because it's the shortest walk.
🚶
Belvedere south of Blaine / north of Big Springs
Houses on Shady Grove, Maricopa, E Campus View Dr, and Nisbet run roughly a 15–20 minute walk to Orbach. W Campus View Dr leads toward Blaine and Watkins.
⛰️
Belvedere Heights
A subsection of Belvedere, just up a hill on the north side of Blaine. Belvedere Heights is around a 6–7 minute scooter ride to the SRC, or roughly a 30-minute walk.

The Blaine/Watkins area sits by North District, which forms the border between Belvedere and Belvedere Heights. Because Belvedere is the closest and most convenient cluster, it's also where competition is fiercest — Belvedere, Belvedere Heights, and the Watkins houses get locked up first every cycle.

Best Bang for Buck

Mission Grove

Mission Grove sits south of Canyon Crest, about a 10–12 minute drive to Lot 30. It's best for students with a car, and it's arguably the best value near UCR — one of the nicest, quietest neighborhoods with the most to do around it, without the premium Belvedere prices. It's a calmer, more residential feel, which suits students who don't mind driving to campus in exchange for a nicer area and lower rent.

Quieter, Car-Friendly

Canyon Crest

Canyon Crest is south of Lot 30. It can be scooter-distance for some, but it's really best for students with cars, scooters, or bikes. The area right next to the country club skews quieter, with an older-resident demographic and fewer students — good if you want a calmer street and don't mind being a bit further out.

City Living

Downtown Riverside

Downtown is for students who want an apartment further from campus, with more of the Riverside scene and less of the UCR bubble. There are some houses out there too, and depending on the exact area it can be cheaper. University Ave is mostly apartments — but the further down University you go, the less nice and less safe it gets, so location within downtown matters.

Other Options

Iowa Ave, Moreno Valley & Spring Mountain Ranch

🏢
Iowa Ave
Mainly apartments and condos — it's a commercial zone, so there are no single-family houses, and it leads toward industrial areas the further down you go. For a purely residential, safer feel, Belvedere is the better bet.
🚗
Moreno Valley (across the 215)
There's a pocket across the 215 from the far end of Canyon Crest that's technically Moreno Valley. Some students go there because it's a little cheaper, but landlords tend to be less student-flexible and more owner-occupied, so loud students can draw noise complaints. The 215 also gets badly packed at peak hours in that section specifically — if you're in a rush you may need to route around past Canyon Crest Country Club, because the Watkins exit off the 215 backs up.
🏡
Highgrove / Spring Mountain Ranch
A newer HOA development north of all the other neighborhoods, good for students who want a bigger house and don't mind driving to campus. It can be pricey relative to its distance — and because the developer is still building, parts of the neighborhood sit next to warehouses and are mid-revamp. Mission Grove isn't as new but is an equally nice neighborhood with more to do nearby, so compare the two if you're considering SMR.
Quick Answers

Which area is right for you?

🚶
Want to walk to campus?
Belvedere off Big Springs and Watkins — about a 12–14 minute walk to Orbach, right by Glen Mor and Pentland.
🚗
Have a car?
Mission Grove — nicest, best bang for buck, quietest, and without the commercial zones you get around Spring Mountain Ranch.
🤫
Want quieter living?
Mission Grove or Canyon Crest near the country club — older demographic, fewer students.
👥
Renting a house with a group?
Belvedere.
💰
Looking for cheaper options?
Moreno Valley or Mission Grove.
🔥
Most competitive areas?
Belvedere, Belvedere Heights, and the Watkins houses — these go first every cycle.
On "walking distance": Under 30 minutes is realistically the cutoff for walking without a car. The sweet spot is around 20 minutes, which is roughly a sub-5-minute scooter ride. If you have a scooter, about 10 minutes is the practical cutoff.
