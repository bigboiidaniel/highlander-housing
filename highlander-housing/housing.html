How UCR Off-Campus Housing Works

Everything you need to know before starting your housing search near UC Riverside. First-time renters, read this first.

The Basics

Life off campus after freshman year

Most UCR students live in the dorms or on-campus apartments during their first year. By sophomore year, the majority move to private off-campus rentals — usually sharing a house or ADU with other students.

The off-campus housing market near UCR is almost entirely made up of private homes rented by individual landlords. There's no single centralized listing system — which is why Highlander Housing exists.

Lease Types

Full house lease vs. renting a room

🏠 Full House Lease

Your group signs one lease for the entire house. You split the rent among yourselves. All roommates typically sign together, and everyone is responsible for the total rent.

Common rent: $3,000–$5,500/mo for a 4–6BR house

🛏️ Renting a Room

Some landlords rent individual rooms in a shared house, each with their own lease. You pay only for your room. You may not choose your housemates.

Common rent: $800–$1,200/mo per room

Timing

When to start looking

This is the thing most students get wrong. UCR housing moves faster than most people expect. If you start looking in April for a September move-in, the best listings will already be gone.

❄️
Jan–Feb
Start looking now. Best selection available.
🌸
Mar–Apr
Still good options, moving fast.
☀️
May–Jun
Limited selection. Most quality listings signed.
🔥
Jul–Aug
Very late. Mostly leftovers at higher prices.
What to Expect

Typical costs near UCR

🏠
Rent
Shared houses typically run $3,000–$5,500/mo for a 4–6BR. Divided among roommates, that's roughly $800–$1,100 per person. ADUs and smaller units can run lower.
🔒
Security Deposit
Usually 1–2 months' rent upfront. In California, landlords can charge up to 2 months for unfurnished units, 3 months for furnished. This is refundable after move-out (minus deductions).
📋
Application Fee
Most landlords charge $30–$65 per applicant for a credit and background check. These fees are non-refundable and paid directly to the landlord — not to Highlander Housing.
💡
Utilities
Some rentals include water and trash. Electricity, gas, and internet are usually tenant responsibility. Budget an extra $80–$150/mo per person if not included.
🚗
Parking
Some properties include driveway parking. Others charge extra or have limited street parking. Always confirm before applying, especially if you have a car.
Applying

What landlords look for

Most UCR students don't have established credit or rental history. Landlords know this — but they still need some assurance. Here's what most landlords ask for:

📄
Co-signer / guarantor

Usually a parent willing to be legally responsible for rent if the student doesn't pay.

🪪
Photo ID

Government-issued ID for each applicant.

📊
Proof of income or bank statements

Either from the student or the co-signer. Usually last 2–3 months of bank statements or pay stubs.

✅
Credit / background check

Most landlords run these. The application fee covers this cost.
