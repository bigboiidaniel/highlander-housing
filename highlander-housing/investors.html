The Market

Why UCR student housing?

UC Riverside has 26,000+ enrolled students and is one of the fastest-growing campuses in the UC system. Most students only live on campus during their freshman year — the rest need off-campus housing.

The result is predictable, high-occupancy rental demand in a defined geographic area. Student renters also tend to form groups (4–6 students splitting a house), which means landlords rent to a group with co-signing parents behind them.

The Riverside market has historically offered lower acquisition prices than coastal markets, while rental yields remain strong relative to purchase price.

Parent Purchase Strategy

Buy instead of rent — the parent investor angle

Some UCR parents — particularly those paying $800–$1,100/month in rent for their student — explore buying a property near UCR instead. If structured correctly, the student lives in one room while other rooms are rented to roommates, offsetting the mortgage.

💡
Equity builds while your student attends
Instead of paying rent for 4 years with no return, a parent purchase generates equity appreciation plus potential rental income from roommates during the student's tenure.
🔄
Built-in exit at graduation
After graduation, the property can be sold (hopefully at a gain) or held as a rental investment using Highlander Housing to fill it.
🌏
Foreign national lending available
Non-US residents and international parents can access specialized loan products for US real estate. We can connect you with lenders who specialize in this space.
